Data standard and digital requirements for education, health and care (EHC) plans

From - Department for Education

Published 15 August 2024

Updated: 24 August 2024 - See all updates

Form a team

Work in partnership with your internal and external stakeholders to digitise your EHCP processes. You will need a designated project lead, preferably an internal SEND senior lead, with IT and information governance representatives advising and influencing where needed.

You should work with your local authority:

  • IT departments and procurement team
  • SEND leads and case workers
  • Information governance representatives
  • educational psychologists
  • social workers
  • integrated care board (ICB)

Form your team and decide roles and responsibilities using a RACI matrix (responsible, accountable, consulted and informed).

Include representatives that have the skills and working knowledge of the internal processes required to manage EHC record workflow and tasks.

Your integrated care board will be actively involved in all SEN improvement projects not just EHCP processes.

Tasks you can do together

Working together on specific tasks will help you create a stronger business case for digitising your EHCP processes and confirm your need for a digital system. Work together on:

  • identifying a need
  • writing a business case
  • getting sign-off for the business case
  • reviewing supplier offers and award (supported by the LA procurement team)

User research

Do user research to understand your users’ needs. You could gather feedback and insight on EHCP processes by talking to:

  • SENDIASS
  • parent and carer forums
  • parents and carers
  • children and young people
  • local SENCOs or headteachers
  • local early years providers
  • local FE training colleges
  • local healthcare professionals (GPs, paediatricians, health visitors, occupational health)

Benefits of working in partnership

Working in partnership supports:

  • a culture of openness
  • shared project goals
  • good leadership
  • positive working relationships
