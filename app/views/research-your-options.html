Research your options

Spend time researching your software and system options. Consider all options available to you, including:

  • preferred software suppliers
  • building your own system
  • a bolt on to your existing system(s)
  • updating your system to meet minimum functionality

Use our guidance on understanding your local authority’s requirements to help you identify what digital EHCP system option will fit with your:

  • ways of working
  • plans for future growth and development

Digital system considerations

  • which option gives you a suitable foundation that you can continue to develop and improve
  • if people can use the system in a way that meets their needs, especially if extra support is needed (For example, is it based on user-centred design and consider universal barriers)
  • how you will recruit and retain the staff to support a system
  • how you will migrate existing data to the new system and what digital solutions best support data migration
  • suppliers’ interest and commitment to providing continued system developments
  • minimum functionality and which option will help you manage and meet these requirements
  • how required changes are managed (For example, system updates, fixes, new features)
  • where data will be stored and how you can manage access
  • any impact on information sharing agreements and DPIA and GDPR rules
  • what options exist for integration with existing systems
  • how you can minimise the burden of data entry and any duplication of effort

Behavioural and cultural considerations

Consider behavioural or cultural factors which might influence your decision. Which option will support or potentially detract from implementing a digital EHCP system that will meet your user and service needs.

You should consider:

  • any cultural barriers to information sharing
  • workforce levels of confidence in using a new system and processes
  • poor data capture practices
  • lack of existing data structures

Talk to others

Consider and communicate what problems you are looking to solve and give potential or existing suppliers, developers or IT teams the opportunity to talk through their offer and any solutions.

Join our local authority community group and speak to other local authorities to get a comprehensive view of what is on offer and how it works in practice.

Read the guidance on Talking to suppliers before you buy and conduct pre-tender market engagement.

Ask for a system demonstration

You should make sure suppliers or developers give you a demonstration of their system. You will need to make sure your key stakeholders are there to ask questions or raise any issues.

Providers should demonstrate how their offering:

  • works across different devices and browsers
  • supports offline working
  • meets WCAG 2.2AA accessibility standards
  • meets The Public Sector Bodies Accessibility Regulations 2018
  • works with assistive technologies, such as screen readers, magnifiers or voice to text
