Data standard and digital requirements for education, health and care (EHC) plans

From - Department for Education

Published 15 August 2024

Updated: 24 August 2024 - See all updates

Understand your requirements

Before you start the digitalisation of your EHCP processes, you need to know:

  • what your current systems, processes and ways of working are
  • want you want and need to improve based on user needs
  • the risks and challenges and plan for these

Your digital EHCP processes will have to:

  • meet the EHCP data standard
  • use the EHCP data schema
  • use the standard EHCP templates
  • meet EHCP minimum requirements for system functionality.

How things work now (current ways of working/your ‘as-is’)

Work in partnership and involve all key stakeholders in mapping out your current EHCP processes and the time and resource needed to complete these processes.

Include IT and information governance colleagues, your integrated care board (ICB), education and healthcare professionals as well as caseworkers, SEND leads, SENDIASS and educational psychologists.

You will need to:

  • understand how your EHCP processes and systems have developed over time
  • understand your existing infrastructure (who does what, how and at what stage of the process)
  • know how existing systems use and store data and how you want to improve and manage this moving forward
  • understand your internal and external reporting requirements (For example, SEN2)
  • gather existing user needs, validate and update where needed
  • know how end users interact with existing systems, including what works well and what doesn’t work well
  • consider how online and offline processes interact and work with each other
  • identify ways digital services can improve ways of working and better meet people’s needs
  • consider things that are out of your control that may stop, slow down or limit the digitalization of your EHCP processes

Information governance

Some processes take longer than others and have multiple stakeholders. Consider in detail, the time needed to agree and sign off:

  • data sharing agreements
  • privacy notices between local authorities and NHS information governance professionals
  • parental consent that aligns with healthcare standards of consent

How you want things to work in the future (assess your needs)

What does success look like for internal stakeholders and your end users.

Parents, carers, healthcare and education professionals. Those that need to use and access the product:

  • Usability - make sure your system can work on mobile devices
  • Healthcare and education professionals need to be able to upload, write or add reports
  • Make it easy and make users feel supported - some SEND leads/local authority representatives lack confidence, knowledge and experience of digital projects

Notifications/alerts need to be clear for users:

  • Healthcare and education professionals need to know who/what the any alerts refer to
  • DCOs get alerts for every transaction/update on a request, so they cannot distinguish the ones they need to address ASAP – can alerts be prioritised/queued?
  • Parents/carers with multiple children with an EHCP don’t know which child the notifications are concerning

    • If a healthcare or education professional changes job and a child needs to transfer to a new person, this has to be done by individual child. This can be time consuming if they support a large number of children (LAs said the same thing).
